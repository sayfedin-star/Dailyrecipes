image: "../../assets/images/salad.webp"
prepTime: 15
cookTime: 0
servings: 4

category: "Salad"
tags: ["salad", "greek", "healthy", "vegetarian", "easy"]
ingredients:
  - "3 large ripe tomatoes, cut into wedges"
  - "1 English cucumber, sliced into thick half-moons"
  - "1 red onion, thinly sliced into rings"
  - "1 green bell pepper, chopped into bite-sized pieces"
  - "1/2 cup Kalamata olives, pitted"
  - "7 oz block of high-quality feta cheese"
  - "1 tsp dried oregano"
  - "4 tbsp extra virgin olive oil"
  - "1 tbsp red wine vinegar"
  - "Pinch of sea salt"
steps:
  - "In a large shallow bowl or platter, arrange the tomato wedges, cucumber slices, green pepper pieces, and red onion rings."
  - "Scatter the Kalamata olives evenly over the top of the vegetables."
  - "Place the block of feta cheese directly on top of the salad (traditional Greek style), or crumble it gently over the salad if preferred."
  - "Whisk the extra virgin olive oil and red wine vinegar in a small bowl, then drizzle it evenly over the entire salad."
  - "Sprinkle the dried oregano and a pinch of sea salt over the feta cheese and vegetables."
  - "Serve immediately at room temperature with crusty bread to soak up the delicious juices."
---

Horiatiki, or traditional Greek Salad, is eaten all over Greece during summer. Unlike Western versions, authentic Greek salad does not contain lettuce. The key to the best Greek salad is using high-quality ingredients, particularly a good extra virgin olive oil and a block of sheep's milk feta cheese rather than pre-crumbled varieties.
